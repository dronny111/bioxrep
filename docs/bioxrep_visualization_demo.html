4. Benchmark Showdown

On the hard alias_symbol task, the structured oracle is the ceiling and pretrained SapBERT is the only learned method above the lexical floor. Two from-scratch surface encoders — a character CNN and a byte-level Transformer trained under an identical regime — both fall below that floor, isolating architecture as the control.

Reading the chart

Both from-scratch students learned their training distribution, but short unseen alias symbols carry little semantic context and little substring overlap with the approved symbol. Swapping convolution for self-attention does not close the gap — the byte-Transformer lands even lower, so the limit is the from-scratch surface signal, not the specific architecture.

Takeaway: pretrained biomedical semantics are the only learned method above the lexical floor on this held-out notation; the architecture control rules out a convolution-specific failure.

5. HGVS Numeric Half

Protein and nucleotide HGVS notations add numeric structure. The hard setting removes easy random-fill decoys, forcing the encoder to map the amino-acid change to the correct codon change.
